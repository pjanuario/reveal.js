Monolith (+)

  • Lower entry cost
  • Fast development cycles in short term
  • Centralised code base
  • Cross cutting functionalities

Microservices (+)

  • Fast development cycles in med/long term
  • Components via services
  • Strong boundaries
  • Independent deploys
  • Decentralised data management

Monolith (+)

  • Easier to build integration&system tests
  • Centralised data management

Microservices (+)

  • Technology diversity
  • Distributed nature is easier to scale
  • Designed for failure
  • Smart endpoints
  • Small codebases
  • Evolutionary design

Monolith (-)

  • Fast development cycles in short term
  • Components via modules or libraries
  • Large codebases
  • Small changes == large deploys
  • Slow feedback

Microservices (-)

  • Higher entry cost
  • Fast development cycles in med/long term
  • Independent deploys
  • Operational overhead
  • Decentralised data management

Monolith (-)

  • Deploys are scheduled and often delayed
  • Hard & expensive to scale
  • Difficult onboarding
  • No sense of ownership

Microservices (-)

  • Technology diversity
  • Distributed nature is harder to build
  • Design for failure
  • System tests are more complex
